What the visualization is showing

How the visualization is interactive

Who the intended audience is

This visualization shows information about the average cost and schedule     variances for the projects in different departments. The variances are shown in percentage and allow a person to know departments whose projects are always completed on time or earlier. It also shows if departments complete their projects with the budgeted cost or not. The blue bars represent the schedule variance       while the orange bars represent the cost variance. Bars with positive values show that a department did not either complete their projects within the specified time       or spent more than budgeted for the project, depending on the color of the bar. The visualization allows a person to hover over the bars, which will show the average cost variance or schedule variance of completed projects per department. The visualization allows a person to be able to view the cost variance alone, the schedule variance alone or both at a time by clicking the       orange or blue square at the top right side of the visualization. Clicking the square would make it dim and disable the bar. This visualization is intended for people that want to compare different departments wit respect to their cost and schedule overruns. At a glance, it shows which departments are able to manage their resources efficiently. Someone can easily pick out which departments are doing well and which departments need to be investigated to find the root cause of the problem
